School vs. Self-Taught

Things I Heard in School

  • "You're completely worthless."
  • "You won’t succeed without a diploma."
  • "Your peers are better than you."
  • "You're not a genius."
  • "Your grades define your potential."
  • "You’re too old and it’s too late to start over."

Things I Heard While Self-Teaching

  • "How did you learn that on your own?"
  • "You picked that up really fast."
  • "This is impressive work."
  • "You’re incredibly talented."
  • "Anyone can learn anything with curiosity."
  • "It’s never too late to start something new."

School vs. Work

Traditional Schooling

  • Focuses on memorization for exams
  • Operates on rigid schedules
  • Encourages one "right" answer
  • Discourages mistakes
  • Fosters unhealthy competition
  • Defines your future in four years
  • Is expensive
  • Requires years of attendance, but learning is optional — grades are mandatory
  • Pressures you to keep up or fall behind
  • Relies on external motivation and approval
  • Labels and limits individuals
  • Creates social divides
  • Encourages conformity over creativity
  • Prioritizes others' opinions
  • Leads to regret and burnout
  • Demands perfection and punishes failure

Self-Taught / Real-World Learning

  • Applies knowledge to real-world problems
  • Allows for flexibility and creativity
  • Supports multiple valid approaches
  • Views mistakes as essential to growth
  • Encourages collaboration
  • Empowers you to reinvent yourself anytime
  • Can be free — and earns money instead of costing it
  • Focuses on learning, not grades — often faster and more efficient
  • Progress is self-paced — there's no "behind"
  • Builds self-confidence and independence
  • Respects individuality
  • Strengthens relationships and teamwork
  • Celebrates passion and uniqueness
  • Puts your needs and goals first
  • Encourages joy in learning
  • Leads to self-worth and fulfillment
  • Fosters freedom and personal meaning

Current System vs. A Better Way

The Current Path

  • At 18, you're expected to go to college — often without knowing why.
  • You’re asked, “What do you want to do for four years?” — not, “What do you want to do with your life?”
  • If your grades are bad, you may be locked out of opportunities — even if you're passionate about them.
  • If you succeed, it becomes your permanent career — even if you're unhappy.
  • If you fail, you're judged harshly, even though failure is often the beginning of true growth.
  • Emotional tolls like depression and anxiety are ignored — or worse, normalized.
  • You're judged for being different, and your life is shaped by people who don’t know you.
  • Conformity is rewarded. Individuality is punished.

A Healthier Alternative

  • At 18, you're asked: “What excites you?” or “What kind of life do you want to build?”
  • Grades don’t define your potential — effort, curiosity, and passion do.
  • If your goal requires school and you’re motivated, pursue it. If not, self-teach or explore alternatives.
  • If you're failing in school, it’s not the end — it may be the beginning of something better.
  • You can still build a meaningful life outside of traditional education.
  • Success becomes personal and diverse — not tied to wealth, status, or grades.
  • There's no shame in switching paths, taking breaks, or doing things your own way.
  • People thrive by doing what they love and what they’re naturally good at — and society benefits from that joy and excellence.
